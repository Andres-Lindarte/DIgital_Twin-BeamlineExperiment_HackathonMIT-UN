Introduction:

These simulations verify the electrostatic and magnetic radius of
refraction (orbit diameters) for non-relativistic and relativistic
ions.

Preparation:

There is no preparation required.  User programs are used to create
the required radial magnetic and electrostatic fields.

Magnetic Radius of Refraction:

Load the file magnetic_orbits.iob into view and click the Fly'm button.

One relativistic electron and two non-relativistic ions are flown.
Expected orbit diameters and frequencies are compared with calculated
values.  The B field is in the positive z direction.


Electrostatic Radius of Refraction:

User programs are used to create an electrostatic field that is always
normal to the ion's current velocity.  Although this is a physically
impossible field to have in reality, it is an ideal and easy way to
compute orbit diameters and frequency as directly as with a magnetic
equivalent.

Load the file electrostatic_orbits.iob into view and click the Fly'm button.

One relativistic electron, two non-relativistic negative ions and two
non-relativistic positive ions are flown.  Expected orbit diameters
and frequencies are compared with calculated values.
