SIMION Secondary Ion Mass Spectromerty (SIMS) Example

Purpose

The files in this directory provide an example of multiple PAs being used to simulate a series of optics. In this case two electrostatic mirrors are used to focus ions from a SIMS target into a device like a quad. The function of these mirrors is to provide chromatic correction of the wide range of ion energies so that a reasonable focus can be obtained.

Files in this demo:

  • README.html - Description of example
  • sims.iob - SIMION workbench
  • sims.fly - SIMION particle definitions associated with sims.iob
  • source.pa* - SIMION potential array - source of particles
  • decel.pa* - SIMION potential array - deceleration
  • mirror.pa* - SIMION potential array - mirror

Usage

How to run the SIMS focusing demo:

  • 1. View sims.iob. (Click View on main screen and select the sims.iob file.)
  • 2. If this is your first time using the example, you will be asked to refine (confirm that and press Refine).
  • 3. Set trajectory quality to 2. (In Particles tab, set TQual to 2.)
  • 4. Fly ions. (Click Fly'm button.)
  • 5. Cut (3D zoom) the exit lens open and view ion impacts. (In ZY view, drag rectangle around lower-left lens and click +Z3D. Switch to XY view, drag rectangle around ions, removing some of the exit lens cage, and click +Z3D again. Switch to 3D Iso view to view.)
  • 6. Look at a PE view of exit lens to see why ions focus the way they do. (In PAs tab, select DECEL.PA0. In Display tab, click Align to align coordinates to this PA. Switch to XY view. Press down PE button.)
  • 7. Look at a PE view of one of the mirrors. (First, ensure PE button is up. In PAs tab, select MIRROR.PA0 or MIRROR.PB0. In Display tab, toggle Align off and on to re-align coordinates to currently selected PA. Switch to XY View. Press down PE button.)
  • 8. Note how the higher energy ions fly further into the mirror and are refracted differently.
  • 9. You change the mirror's voltage to adjust its chromatic correction - interesting! (In PAs tab, select MIRROR.PA0 or MIRROR.PB0. Click Fast Adjust Voltages. Change voltage to some other value and press Fast Adjust. Click Fly'm button to refly.)
  • 10. Experiment.
